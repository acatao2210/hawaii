1.6 mi round trip, ~560 ft gain. Iconic volcanic crater with 360° views of Waikiki and the coast. Mix of paved path, steep stairs, short lighted tunnel. Allow 1.5 hrs. Best in early morning before heat and crowds.
Reserve: hawaiistateparks.org · $5/person + $10 parking. Book weeks ahead.

Koko Head Crater Hike Hike ▾
Only 0.45 miles to the summit, but you'll climb 1,048 old railway tie stairs — 930 feet of elevation gain. Known locally as the "Stairmaster of Death," it's brutal but short. Stunning 360° views of Hanauma Bay, Honolulu, and the Pacific. Go at sunrise for best light and cooler temps. No reservation needed.
Very exposed with no shade — sunscreen and water essential. Extremely hot by 9 am.

Hours
Open daily · Best before 8am to beat the heat
Notes

Waipio Valley Overlook Culture ▾
The "Valley of the Kings" — a sacred site where Hawaiian royalty once lived. The Big Island's most iconic experience — snorkel in the dark off the Kona coast as 8–15 ft wingspan manta rays do barrel rolls directly beneath you, feeding on plankton attracted by underwater lights.
