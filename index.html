Monday, May 18 · Oahu · With Friends
Arrival — First Taste
Land at 1:42 pm · Settle, eat, explore Oahu
Afternoon & Evening
1:42 pm
Arrive HNL Travel
United 219 lands from ORD. Pick up rental car at HNL. Traffic out of the airport can be slow — give yourself time. Drive toward Waikiki or Kailua depending on where you're staying. Don't nap — push through to local bedtime to beat jet lag.
3:00 pm
Check In + Beach Walk Beach
Get oriented. If near Waikiki, stroll the beachfront strip. May in Oahu is warm (~82°F), settled weather, low chance of rain. The sun, humidity, and light will already feel like a different world.
5:30 pm
Dinner: Rainbow Drive-In or Helena's Hawaiian Food Food
Rainbow Drive-In (3308 Kanaina Ave, Kapahulu): Oahu icon since 1961 — loco moco, mixed plates, plate lunches. Open daily till 9 pm. Helena's Hawaiian Food (1240 N School St): Michelin-recognized since 1946 — kalua pig, pipikaula short ribs, butterfish collar. Helena's is closed Mon/Sat/Sun, so this works if arriving Tuesday or later.
8:00 pm
Leonard's Bakery for Malasadas Food
Portuguese donut-style pastry on Kapahulu Ave — crispy outside, choice of fillings: guava, custard, coconut. Open till 7 pm (arrive before close). Cash helps for small orders. The perfect first-night dessert.

Tuesday, May 19 · Oahu · With Friends
Southeast Coast
Diamond Head · Hanauma Bay · Kailua
Morning
6:30 am
Diamond Head Summit Trail Hike
1.6 mi round trip, ~560 ft gain. Iconic volcanic crater with 360° views of Waikiki and the coast. Mix of paved path, steep stairs, short lighted tunnel. Allow 1.5 hrs. Best in early morning before heat and crowds.
Reserve: hawaiistateparks.org · $5/person + $10 parking. Book weeks ahead.
9:00 am
Makapu'u Point Lighthouse Trail Hike
2 mi paved trail, moderate incline up Oahu's southeast cape. Spectacular coastal views, offshore islets, the historic red-roofed lighthouse. Opens 7 am, no reservation needed. Best light in the morning.
Midday
11:30 am
Hanauma Bay Snorkel Adventure
Hawaii's premier protected marine sanctuary — crystal-clear water, diverse tropical fish, frequent sea turtles. Mandatory short educational video on arrival. Gear rentals on-site. Go on the left side of the bay for better fish diversity.
Reserve: hanaumabaystatepark.org — timed slots open 2 days ahead at 7 am. $25/person. Sells out fast.
Afternoon & Evening
3:00 pm
Kailua Beach + Mokulua Islands Kayak Beach
Kailua Beach is arguably the most beautiful beach on Oahu — turquoise, calm, powder sand backed by the Ko'olau mountains. Kayak rentals nearby for paddling out to the twin Mokulua Islands (The Mokes). Allow 2–3 hrs for the full paddle. Arrive early for parking.
7:00 pm
Dinner in Kailua: Faria Food
One of Oahu's best new restaurants. Hawaii-rooted Portuguese cuisine — bean soup, lau lau stew, tinned fish board, pasteis de nata. Warm, intimate. Reservations recommended.

Wednesday, May 20 · Oahu · With Friends
Adventure Day
Koko Crater · Kualoa Ranch · Pali Lookout
Early Morning
6:00 am
Koko Crater Railway Hike Hike
0.8 mi, 912 ft gain, 1,048 railway-tie steps straight up a dormant volcanic crater. Known locally as the "Stairmaster of Death" — brutal but short. Stunning 360° views of Hanauma Bay, Honolulu, and the Pacific at the top. Go at sunrise for best light and cooler temps. No reservation needed.
Very exposed and no shade — sunscreen and water essential. Extremely hot by 9 am.
Late Morning – Afternoon
10:00 am
Kualoa Ranch Adventure
4,000-acre working ranch at the base of the Ko'olau Mountains — filming site for Jurassic Park, LOST, Jumanji, Kong: Skull Island. Choose from ATV/UTV tours, zip-lining, horseback riding, or the movie sites bus tour. The UTV off-road adventure is spectacular. Book at kualoa.com.
Book: kualoa.com — half-day from $199. Allow 3–4 hrs.
3:00 pm
Nu'uanu Pali Lookout Culture
Dramatic windy cliffside 1,200 ft above the Windward Coast. Site of King Kamehameha I's decisive 1795 battle. Short 5-min walk from parking. Extremely windy — hold onto everything loosely. $7 parking fee. One of the most dramatic viewpoints in Hawaii.
Evening
7:00 pm
Dinner: Fete (Downtown Honolulu) Food
Brooklyn-meets-Hawaii bistro in Chinatown. Kualoa Ranch pork specials, carbonara with Portuguese sausage, Korean fried chicken sandwich, local fish of the day. Great cocktails. Open Mon–Sat 11 am–10 pm. Reserve a table.

Thursday, May 21 · Oahu · With Friends
North Shore Day
Ehukai Pillbox · Waimea Valley · Haleiwa · Shave Ice
Morning
7:30 am
Breakfast: Sunrise Shack (North Shore) Food
Cult-favorite North Shore roadside stand. Acai bowls, papaya bowls, coffee drinks. The vibe of the North Shore at sunrise with a bowl in hand is worth the early start.
9:00 am
Ehukai Pillbox Trail Hike
~1 hr round trip, steep but short. Climbs to WWII concrete bunkers with panoramic views of Sunset Beach and the entire North Shore. Park near Sunset Elementary School. Ropes assist the steepest section. Can be muddy after rain — closed-toe shoes.
11:00 am
Waimea Valley & Waimea Falls Hike
Paved 1.5 mi walk through botanical gardens and Hawaiian cultural sites to a swimmable waterfall pool. Life jackets provided for swimming. Check swim conditions ahead — lifeguards decide daily at 9 am. Closed Mon–Tue.
Entry: ~$26/adult. Wed–Sun only. waimeavalley.net
Afternoon & Evening
1:30 pm
North Shore Shrimp Trucks Food
A North Shore rite of passage. Giovanni's is the most famous white truck. Garlic butter shrimp with two scoops of rice is the move. Multiple competing trucks — any with a line is a good choice. Eat at picnic tables. Cash-friendly.
3:00 pm
Haleiwa Town + Matsumoto Shave Ice Culture
Stroll historic Haleiwa — surf shops, galleries, local boutiques. Matsumoto's Shave Ice (66-111 Kamehameha Hwy) is an island institution since 1951. Add ice cream at the bottom and azuki beans. Island Snow across the street is equally excellent.
5:00 pm
Sunset at Sunset Beach Beach
Grab a spot on the sand for one of the best golden-hour sunsets on the island. The North Shore glows in the late afternoon light. No agenda needed — just sit and watch the sky change.

Friday, May 22 · Oahu · With Friends
History & Culture
Pearl Harbor · Chinatown · Manoa Falls · Iolani Palace
Morning
8:00 am
Pearl Harbor National Memorial Culture
One of the most significant WWII sites in the world. The USS Arizona Memorial boat tour is the centerpiece — the ship lies submerged with 1,177 crew members aboard. Budget 3–4 hours. Optional add-ons: Battleship Missouri, Pacific Aviation Museum.
Book: recreation.gov well in advance. Free entry; boat tours require timed tickets.
Afternoon
12:30 pm
Lunch: Chinatown Honolulu Food
Highway Inn for multicultural plate lunches (kalua pig, lau lau, poke, mochiko chicken), or Kapa Hale for chef Keaka Lee's elevated local cooking. Both are on the same stretch of downtown.
2:30 pm
Manoa Falls Trail Hike
Easy 1.6 mi round trip through a lush rainforest valley 15 min from Waikiki. Jurassic Park was filmed here. A beautiful waterfall at the end. Can be muddy — closed-toe shoes a must. Swimming not allowed at the pool.
5:00 pm
Iolani Palace Culture
The only royal palace on US soil — built 1882 by King Kalakaua. Deeply significant for Hawaiian sovereignty history; Queen Lili'uokalani was imprisoned here after the 1893 overthrow. Audio self-guided tours Tue–Sat 9 am–4 pm. Closed Mon/Sun.
Entry ~$22/adult. iolanipalace.org
Evening
7:30 pm
Dinner: MW Restaurant or Nami Kaze Food
MW Restaurant: Pacific Rim cuisine — the mochi-crusted Kona Kanpachi is legendary. Nami Kaze: 2023 James Beard Award semifinalist. Both require reservations — book well ahead.

HNL → KOA (Kona) · Settle in · First evening solo

See friends off at HNL Travel

Afternoon
Check in + Explore Kailua-Kona Culture
Kailua-Kona is the west-side hub — restaurants, shopping, Kailua Bay waterfront. Stroll Alii Drive along the water, visit Ahu'ena Heiau (sacred Hawaiian temple at the edge of the bay), and get your bearings. Kona is drier and sunnier than Hilo.

Sunset at Kailua Bay Beach
West-facing Kona coast means incredible sunsets directly over the Pacific ocean — no haze, no islands in the way. Grab a drink from Huggo's On the Rocks or Lava Lava Beach Club, find a spot at the water's edge, and watch the sky turn.

Guy Fieri-approved Kona institution. Famous for fresh poke bowls — the avo poke nachos are legendary. Also: ahi fish tacos, garlic furikake chicken, Hawaiian plate lunches, excellent mai tais. Open 11 am–9 pm daily. Best poke on the Kona side.

Monday, May 25 · Big Island · Solo
Hawai'i Volcanoes + South Island
Active lava · Lava tube · Black sand beach · Night glow
The Big Island contains Kīlauea, one of the most active volcanoes on Earth. Check nps.gov/havo for current eruption and trail status before you go — conditions change. May 2025–2026 has seen active eruptions on and off. The park is spectacular regardless.
Early Morning – Drive
7:00 am
Drive Kona → Hawai'i Volcanoes NP Travel
~2 hour drive from Kona south through Ka'ū to the park entrance. The drive itself is striking — black lava fields giving way to cloud forest. Pack layers; it's cooler at elevation (~4,000 ft). Bring water and snacks, limited food inside the park.
Entry: $30/vehicle, valid 7 days. Pay at the gate or in advance at recreation.gov.
Morning – Park Activities
9:30 am
Crater Rim Trail + Kīlauea Overlook Hike
Walk the rim of the Kīlauea Caldera — one of the most surreal landscapes on earth. Steam vents, sulfur banks, and views into the massive volcanic crater. Various sections open depending on activity level. The Kīlauea Visitor Center is your first stop for current conditions.
11:00 am
Nāhuku (Thurston) Lava Tube Adventure
A short, stunning hike begins in one of the wettest rainforests in the park before descending into a massive underground lava tube — you walk in one end and out the other. The lit section is ~0.4 miles and takes about 30 minutes. An eerie, otherworldly experience unlike anything else in Hawaii.
1:00 pm
Chain of Craters Road Adventure
A dramatic 19-mile road descending from 4,000 ft through vast hardened lava flows all the way to the ocean. Multiple overlooks, petroglyphs site (Pu'u Loa), and the point where old lava crossed the road and stopped. Ends at the sea. One of the most dramatic drives in the US.
Afternoon
3:30 pm
Punalu'u Black Sand Beach Beach
One of the most unique beaches in the world — jet black volcanic sand beach where endangered Hawaiian green sea turtles (honu) regularly haul out to rest on the sand. Free entry, free parking. Don't touch or approach the turtles. Snorkeling possible but rough. 30 min from Volcanoes NP.
Evening
After dark
Return to Kīlauea for night glow (if erupting) Adventure
If Kīlauea is actively erupting (check nps.gov/havo), return after dark for the lava glow — the caldera turns orange and red against a black night sky. It's one of the most primal, moving natural spectacles you can witness. The park is open 24 hrs. Drive time from Punalu'u: ~30 min.
Check eruption status at: nps.gov/havo/planyourvisit/lava2.htm before heading back.
Late
Dinner: The Rim at Volcano House or drive back to Kona Food
Volcano House (inside the park, on the crater rim) serves dinner with views of the caldera — atmospheric, especially at night during an eruption. Or drive back to Kona (~2 hrs) for Kona Brewing Co. or another local spot. It's a long but unforgettable day.

Tuesday, May 26 · Big Island · Solo
North Island + Manta Rays
Waipio Valley · Pololu · 'Akaka Falls · Manta ray night snorkel
Early Morning – Drive North
7:00 am
Waipio Valley Overlook Culture
The "Valley of the Kings" — a sacred site where Hawaiian royalty once lived. From the lookout: a panorama of impossibly steep green valley walls, a black sand beach below, and a waterfall plunging from the cliffs. The road down is one of the steepest in the US (25% grade) and requires a 4WD vehicle. The overlook itself is spectacular and free.
Note: Hiking down is sensitive — the valley is a sacred Hawaiian place. Check current access guidance before attempting. Tour shuttles (~$70) are the respectful way to access the valley floor.
Mid Morning
9:30 am
'Akaka Falls State Park Hike
Easy 0.4 mi paved loop through a lush rainforest of giant bamboo, tropical flowers, and towering 'ōhi'a trees — ending at a viewpoint of 'Akaka Falls, a stunning 442-foot single-drop waterfall into the lush gorge below. One of the best easy hikes in Hawaii. Opens 8:30 am.
$10 parking at lot (or park on road for free). 30 min north of Hilo.
11:30 am
Lunch in Hilo: Moon and Turtle or Hilo Bay Cafe Food
Moon and Turtle (51 Kalakaua St): intimate, acclaimed restaurant with inventive ocean-to-table small plates — smokey sashimi, truffle mushroom risotto, ono ceviche. Closed Mon/Sun; Tue–Sat 5:30 pm+ for dinner, but check if lunch is available. Hilo Bay Cafe is the upscale waterfront lunch option with sushi, fresh fish, and views of Mauna Kea on a clear day.
Afternoon
1:30 pm
Pololu Valley Lookout + Hike Hike
At the very north tip of the island — drive along the dramatic Kohala Coast cliffs to the lookout, then hike 0.9 mi (round trip) down steep switchbacks to the isolated black sand beach and valley floor. Steep and can be slippery — wear real shoes. Stunning remote beauty, rarely crowds. The beach is not swimmable but the landscape is extraordinary.
Evening – Signature Experience
7:00 pm
Manta Ray Night Snorkel Adventure
The Big Island's most iconic experience — snorkel in the dark off the Kona coast as 8–15 ft wingspan manta rays do barrel rolls directly beneath you, feeding on plankton attracted by underwater lights. They come within inches. No stingers, no danger. Guides hold a board you float on. It's surreal. Available most nights year-round; May conditions are excellent.
Book: Multiple operators in Kona — Manta Ray Night Snorkel (74-5583 Luhia St) has a 5.0-star rating. ~$100–130/person. Book weeks ahead.
After
Late dinner: Kona Brewing Co. or Merriman's Food
Kona Brewing Co. (74-5612 Pawai Pl) is open till 9 pm and perfect post-snorkel — fresh poke, pizza, Cubano sandwich, draft Kona ales brewed on-site. Or if you booked ahead: Merriman's in Waimea (Upcountry) is the most acclaimed restaurant on the Big Island — farm-to-table Hawaiian cuisine, 90% local ingredients, incredible. Worth the drive.

Afternoon

If you skipped the Lanikai Pillbox on Day 6, this is your chance — WWII bunkers with jaw-dropping views over turquoise Lanikai Beach and the Mokulua Islands. Best in late afternoon light. Park at Kailua Beach Park, 15 min walk to trailhead.
