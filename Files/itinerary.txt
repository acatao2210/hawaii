DAY 2 — Tuesday, May 19
Diamond Head + Culture
Hotel: Waikiki / Kailua

  6:30 am  Diamond Head State Monument
           1.6 mi round trip · ~560 ft gain · Allow 90 min
           BOOK at hawaiistateparks.org — $5/person + $10 parking (sells out)
           Volcanic crater · 360° views of Waikiki and coast · stairs + lighted tunnel

  9:30 am  Bishop Museum (1525 Bernice St, Honolulu)
           Open 9am–5pm · $38.95/adult · Allow 2–3 hrs
           World's largest Hawaiian artifact collection · Hawaiian Hall, Polynesian exhibits

  1:00 pm  Lyon Arboretum (3860 Manoa Rd) — Mon–Fri 9am–3pm only
           Free · 200-acre tropical botanical garden · native Hawaiian plants, orchids, palms

  3:30 pm  Manoa Falls Trail
           1.6 mi round trip · $5 parking · Closed Mondays
           150-ft waterfall, lush rainforest · Muddy — waterproof closed-toe shoes required

  6:30 pm  DINNER: Chinatown Honolulu
           Highway Inn (680 Iwilei Rd): plate lunches, kalua pig, lau lau · Mon–Sat 9am–8pm
           OR Kapa Hale (50 N Hotel St): elevated local cooking, chef Keaka Lee

  Driving:  ~16 mi · ~55 min

DAY 3 — Wednesday, May 20
Southeast Coast — Hanauma Bay & Kailua
Hotel: Waikiki / Kailua

  7:00 am  Makapu'u Point Lighthouse Trail (east tip of O'ahu)
           2 mi round trip · Paved · Free · No reservation needed
           Open 7am–7:45pm · Arrive early (parking fills by 8am)
           Panoramic views of windward coast, Rabbit Island, historic lighthouse

  9:30 am  Waiāhole Beach Park (windward coast, Kamehameha Hwy)
           Free · Quick stop — black volcanic sand, uncrowded, no facilities

  11:00 am Hanauma Bay Nature Preserve
           BOOK 2 days ahead at 7:00am exactly: hanaumabaystatepark.org
           $25/person · Open Wed–Sun only (CLOSED Mon & Tue)
           Snorkel gear rental ~$25 on site · Mandatory 9-min conservation video
           Protected crater bay — tropical fish, sea turtles, calm water

  3:00 pm  Kailua Beach + Mokulua Islands Kayak
           Beach: Free · Kayak: ~$30–50/hr (Twogood Kayaks, Kailua Beach Rd)
           Best beach on O'ahu — powdery white sand, turquoise water, Ko'olau backdrop
           Kayak 1 mi offshore to the twin Moke Islands · Allow 2–3 hrs

  7:00 pm  DINNER: Faria (Kailua Town)
           Hawaii-rooted Portuguese cuisine · ~$20–35/person
           Bean soup, lau lau stew, pasteis de nata · Reservations required

  Driving:  ~51 mi · ~1 hr 35 min

DAY 4 — Thursday, May 21
Adventure + Windward Coast
Hotel: Waikiki / Kailua

  4:30 pm  Nu'uanu Pali Lookout

DAY 9 — Monday, May 25
North Loop — Akaka Falls & Waipio → Hilo
Hotel: Hilo (Night 3)

  [Morning] Drive Kona → Waipio Valley via Saddle Road
            ~1 hr 30 min · ~75 mi · Scenic cross-island highway

  [~10am]  Waipio Valley Lookout (Hamakua Coast)
           Free · No hiking required · 15 min at the rim
           Sheer 2,000-ft green valley walls dropping to black sand beach
           Most sacred valley in Hawaii — birthplace of Kamehameha I's power
           DO NOT drive into the valley with a rental car (25% grade, dangerous)

  [~11am]  Drive south along Hamakua Coast → Honomu
            ~30 min · ~25 mi

  [~11:30] 'Akaka Falls State Park (Honomu, near Hilo)
           Open 8:30am–5pm · $5/vehicle · No reservation
           Paved 0.4-mi loop trail · 442-ft free-fall waterfall in jungle gorge
           Bamboo, heliconia, ginger, tree ferns · 30–45 min · Bring bug spray

  [~1pm]   Drive to Hilo and check in: ~20 min · ~15 mi

  7:00 pm  DINNER in Hilo: Seaside Restaurant
           Mahi mahi, furikake salmon, blackened ahi · Classic Hilo lagoon dining
           OR: Cafe 100 — loco moco (birthplace of the dish) · quick and cheap

  Driving:  ~3 hrs · ~145 mi total

  Note: Head north first while energy is high; Akaka Falls sits between Waipio
  and Hilo on the return — no backtracking.

DAY 10 — Tuesday, May 26
Volcanoes & Mauna Kea
Hotel: Hilo (Night 4)

  7:00 am  Leave Hilo → Hawai'i Volcanoes National Park
           ~30 min · ~30 mi
           Open 24 hrs · $30/vehicle (7-day pass, in-and-out privileges)
           Check NPS air quality before: sulfur dioxide vog varies
           Morning sequence (aim to finish by noon):
             · Kilauea Iki Trail — 4 mi, moderate, hardened lava lake with steam vents
             · Thurston Lava Tube (Nāhuku) — 500-yr-old tunnel, easy 20-min walk
             · Chain of Craters Road — coastal lava field panorama

  12:00 pm Return to Hilo · Rest, lunch
           Cafe 100 (loco moco) or Lava Rock Cafe (Volcano village)
           Ola Brew Co. — local craft beer, outdoor brew yard

  3:00 pm  Drive Hilo → Mauna Kea Visitor Center (~45 min · ~35 mi)

  3:45 pm  Mauna Kea Visitor Center (9,200 ft) — Open 9am–9:30pm
           STOP: acclimatize minimum 30 min before ascending further
           Free public stargazing with telescopes from ~6pm (clear nights)

  4:30 pm  Drive to Summit (13,796 ft) — ~30 min · ~10 mi · Steep slow road
           Summit temperature: ~33°F year-round — bring full warm layers
           Sunset above the cloud layer · Milky Way visible to the naked eye
           CONFIRM: check rental car policy on summit road access first

  [Evening] Drive back to Hilo — ~1 hr · ~45 mi

  DINNER:  WSW Steakhouse (Hilo Hawaiian Hotel) — Prime beef, bay views
           OR: Ola Brew Co. for casual craft beer before the Mauna Kea ascent

  Driving:  ~3 hrs · ~150 mi total

  Note: Leave Hilo by 7am for Volcanoes — morning air is cooler and the
  crater is quieter before tour buses arrive.

South Loop + Mahalo
Hotel: Kona (Night 5, May 27)
Departure: May 28

  MAY 27

  7:30 am  Leave Hilo heading south

  [~8:30]  Punalu'u Black Sand Beach (Ka'ū District) — ~1 hr from Hilo (~55 mi)
           Open 24 hrs · Free parking
           Jet-black volcanic sand · sea turtles resting on shore
           Stay 10+ feet from turtles (federal law)

  [~10:00] Papakōlea Green Sand Beach (South Point, Ka Lae) — ~30 min (~25 mi)
           One of only a handful of green sand beaches on Earth
           Olivine crystal sand inside a collapsed volcanic crater
           HIKE: 2.5 mi each way, exposed and shadeless — bring 2+ liters water per person
           OR: Local truck shuttle from parking area ~$20–25 cash each way (bring cash)

  [Afternoon] Drive north up west coast to Kona — ~1 hr 15 min · ~70 mi

  7:00 pm  DINNER in Kona (final night)
           Huggo's — sunset lanai table, ahi, mahi mahi, prime rib · Book a table
           OR: Quinn's Almost by the Sea — casual bar, ribeye, ono fish tacos, kalbi ribs
           OR: Kona Inn Restaurant — historic oceanfront bar, award-winning Mai Tais

  Driving May 27: ~3 hrs 15 min · ~175 mi
