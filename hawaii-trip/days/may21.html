Diamond Head + Snorkel + Rooftops

The classic crater at sunrise, the world's best snorkeling bay, and Waikiki from above at sunset.

🌋
Diamond Head Crater Hike
Hiking Must-do

The iconic 0.8-mile trail (1.6 miles round trip) up the inside of Diamond Head crater to the summit. You'll climb through tunnels and up spiral staircases before emerging to a 360° view of Waikiki, the Ko'olau Mountains, and the Pacific. Go early — it gets hot and crowded by 9 AM. The morning light on the city below is extraordinary.

Reserve ahead Timed-entry reservations are required at hawaiistateparks.org — book at least a week ahead. Parking is $10. Alternatively, park outside the park and walk in (saves the fee).

Hanauma Bay Snorkel
Beach Must-do

One of the best snorkeling spots in the world. Hanauma Bay is a marine preserve inside a volcanic crater, with incredibly clear water, a shallow protected reef, and more tropical fish than you've ever seen. Sea turtles cruise through regularly. You'll watch a mandatory ocean safety video before entering the water.

Must book ahead Hanauma Bay requires advance reservations at hanaumabaystatepark.com — they sell out weeks in advance during peak season. Reservations open 2 days prior at 7 AM. Arrive before your slot time.

Ono Seafood
Food

A tiny, cash-only poke institution on Kapahulu Avenue. Often considered the best poke on Oahu by locals — the fish is incredibly fresh, portions are generous, and the shoyu ahi is transcendent. Get there right at noon or prepare to wait in line. It's worth every minute.

Order Get the shoyu ahi poke bowl plus the spicy ahi. Ask for furikake on top. Eat it on a bench outside — there's no seating inside. Cash only. Usually runs out of certain fish by 2 PM.

Waikiki Rooftop Bars at Sunset
Nightlife Must-do

Waikiki has a surprisingly good rooftop bar scene. Holopono at the Queen Kapiolani Hotel has unobstructed Diamond Head and ocean views. Zanzabar at the Waikiki Trade Center gets lively. Side Street Inn on Hopaka is a legendary local bar — dark, loud, and beloved. For a special evening, the House Without a Key at the Halekulani is old-school luxury Hawaii with live hula.

Sunset timing Sunset is around 7:15–7:25 PM in late May. Position yourself at a rooftop by 6:45 PM with a drink in hand.
