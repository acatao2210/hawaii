Two-Step Snorkel + Sacred Sites

The last full day. Turtles, rays, and the most sacred place of refuge in Hawaii.

🐠
Two-Step Snorkel (Hōnaunau Bay)
Beach Must-do

Named for the two lava rock steps that drop right into the water, this is one of the best shore-entry snorkel spots in Hawaii. The bay is crystal clear, protected, and absolutely alive with marine life — green sea turtles, spinner dolphins, eagle rays, and hundreds of tropical fish. It's adjacent to Pu'uhonua o Honaunau and completely free. Get there early before the tour boats arrive.

Go early Arrive by 8 AM before the tour groups. The water is calmest in the morning. Fins are helpful — the water gets deeper quickly to the right of the entry point. Respect the turtles — 10-foot distance rule.

Pu'uhonua o Honaunau National Historical Park
Culture

One of the most spiritually significant places in all of Hawaii — a place of refuge where ancient Hawaiians who broke kapu (sacred law) could swim to and receive absolution, escaping death. The reconstructed wooden ki'i (temple images), royal fishponds, and the great wall built without mortar are extraordinary. The NPS has done sensitive, beautiful work preserving this place.

Take your time The audio tour is worth doing — it brings the history alive. Respect the sacred nature of the space. The palms, fishponds, and reconstructed structures make it one of the most atmospheric places in the state.

Big Island Brewhaus — Farewell Lunch
Food

A legendary farm-to-table brewpub in the upcountry town of Waimea, run by an award-winning brewer. The beers are outstanding (try the Overboard IPA or White Mountain Porter) and the food — burgers, fresh fish tacos, house-made sauces — is some of the best pub grub on the island. A perfect farewell to the Big Island.

Waimea Waimea (also called Kamuela) is worth spending an extra hour in — it's a charming paniolo (Hawaiian cowboy) ranching town at 2,600 feet with cool air, farmers markets, and excellent local restaurants. Merriman's restaurant here is one of the founders of the Hawaii Regional Cuisine movement.
