Arrive at Daniel K. Inouye International Airport (HNL)
Culture

You've landed in paradise. Pick up your rental car — you'll need it for North Shore and windward coast days. Honolulu traffic can be slow in the afternoon, so take the H1 west to get to Waikiki.

Pro tip Book your rental car well in advance for May — peak season means inventory goes fast. Consider a compact for in-city days but think about a 4x4 for Big Island lava roads later.

Check In — Waikiki Hotel
Beach

Drop your bags and take a walk down Kalakaua Avenue, the main strip of Waikiki. The beach is right there — dip your feet in and watch the sunset. The vibe shifts from chaotic to magical once the sun goes down.

Where to stay The Outrigger Waikiki, Surfjack Hotel, or Shoreline Hotel are solid mid-range picks with great locations. Avoid the far end of Waikiki — you want to be within walking distance of Kapahulu Avenue for food.

Dinner in Chinatown
Food Nightlife

Honolulu's Chinatown is a 15-minute drive from Waikiki and one of the best dining and bar neighborhoods in the state. It's gritty, lively, and full of great food. Try Lucky Belly for ramen, Pig & the Lady for creative Hawaiian-Asian fusion, or Little Village Noodle House for classic dim sum vibes.

Nightlife After dinner, walk around Hotel Street. The bars here — like M Nightclub, Tchin Tchin, and REAL — are a mix of dive bars, cocktail lounges, and live music spots. Your friends will love it.
