Cafe 100 — Loco Moco
Food

The loco moco is Hawaii's ultimate comfort food — white rice topped with a hamburger patty, a fried egg, and brown gravy. Cafe 100 in Hilo is the legendary birthplace of the dish (though you're still on Oahu today, many local spots do excellent versions). Look for Da Kitchen, Rainbow Drive-In, or any local plate lunch spot near your hotel.

Plate lunch culture The Hawaiian plate lunch — two scoops of rice, macaroni salad, and a protein — is the soul of local food culture. Rainbow Drive-In on Kapahulu Ave does a legendary plate lunch that's been feeding Oahu since 1961.

Afternoon Flexibility
Culture Beach

Use this as a buffer day to revisit favorites, do any last-minute shopping at Ala Moana Center (the largest open-air mall in the US, with great local brands), or simply spend a last afternoon on Waikiki Beach. The Bishop Museum is excellent if you want a deep dive into Hawaiian culture and natural history — easily 3 hours.

Packing for Big Island Tonight or this afternoon, repack for your Big Island adventure. You'll want: layers for Mauna Kea (40°F at the summit), sturdy shoes for lava fields, snorkel gear if you have it, and sunscreen. Leave non-essential luggage at the hotel if you're returning to HNL for your flight home.

Final Group Dinner
Nightlife Food

The last supper with your crew before going solo. Make it special. Vintage Cave is one of Hawaii's finest restaurants — an underground dining room with spectacular wine and tasting menu. For a more casual but equally memorable send-off, MW Restaurant does modern Hawaiian cuisine in a beautiful setting. Or go back to Chinatown for a looser, more festive night.

Reservation For a special farewell dinner, book at MW Restaurant or Senia — both are excellent representations of modern Hawaiian cuisine and require a reservation. Book at least a week ahead.
