Morning Flight — HNL to Hilo (ITO)
Culture

Book the earliest flight you can get from Honolulu to Hilo International Airport on the Big Island. Hawaiian Airlines and Mokulele both serve this route. The flight is about 45 minutes — window seat on the left side for views of Maui and the Big Island coastline on approach.

Car rental Pick up your rental car at Hilo airport. You'll need it for everything on the Big Island — public transport is minimal. The entire island is about the size of Connecticut.

'Akaka Falls State Park
Hiking Must-do

A jaw-dropping 440-foot waterfall plunging into a rainforest gorge — one of the tallest free-fall waterfalls in the US. The loop trail is a gorgeous 0.4-mile paved walk through Hawaiian rainforest with giant bamboo, wild orchids, and ferns. 'Akaka Falls is the grand finale — you'll hear it before you see it.

Details Park entrance is $5/person. The trail is entirely paved and easy. About 15 miles north of Hilo — combine with a stop at the charming town of Honomu on the way, which has some good local shops.

Hilo Farmers Market
Culture Food

One of the best farmers markets in Hawaii, held Wednesday and Saturday. Local vendors sell Big Island tropical fruit (rambutan, longan, star fruit, breadfruit), fresh-cut flowers, local honey, macadamia nuts, and ready-to-eat food. It's a great place to taste things you've never tried and stock up on snacks for the next few days.

What to buy Buy a bag of Kona coffee direct from a grower — much cheaper than stores. Also look for Big Island-grown vanilla, local chocolate, and a bunch of whatever tropical fruit looks best. The breakfast burritos from the food stalls are solid.

Rainbow Falls (Waiānuenue)
Must-do

A stunning 80-foot waterfall that flows into a natural pool, framed by tropical vegetation. In the morning mist, a rainbow often forms in the spray below the falls — hence the name. It's right in Hilo town, easy to access, and usually uncrowded in the afternoon. Walk up the stairs behind the viewpoint to see the ancient lava tube at the top.

Timing Morning visits (before 10 AM on sunny days) are when the rainbow effect is most visible. But afternoon visits are less crowded and still beautiful. Free entry.

Suisan Fish Market — Fresh Poke
Food

Hilo's legendary fish market, open since 1907. Their poke counter is outstanding — fresh-caught local ahi, octopus (tako), and salmon in a dozen preparations. Get a pound of poke and a scoop of rice, find a picnic table near the Hilo waterfront, and watch the fishing boats come in. This is the Big Island.

Close early Suisan closes around 5 PM on Saturdays — don't dawdle. If you miss it, try Poke Express or Kuhio Grille in town.
