Tantalus + Local Food Day

A lush jungle waterfall, the best city views on the island, and Oahu's most beloved food stops.

💧
Manoa Falls Hike
Hiking

A beautiful, jungle-dense 1.6-mile round-trip trail through Manoa Valley to a 150-foot waterfall. The trail passes through one of the most lush rainforests on Oahu — massive banyan trees, wild ginger, and the smell of green everywhere. The falls are dramatic after rain. Get there early before the tour groups arrive.

Be aware The trail can be muddy — wear shoes you don't mind getting dirty. Don't swim under the falls — leptospirosis risk from the freshwater. Check for flash flood warnings if it's been raining heavily.

Pu'u 'Ualaka'a State Wayside (Tantalus Lookout)
Must-do

The best panoramic view of Honolulu — and one that barely anyone outside of locals knows about. From a cinder cone at 1,000 feet, you can see Diamond Head, Waikiki, downtown Honolulu, Pearl Harbor, and the Manoa Valley all at once. The winding drive up Round Top Drive through the rainforest is half the experience. Perfect spot for a late morning picnic.

Hidden gem This is genuinely one of the best views on Oahu and almost never crowded. Go on a clear morning (May mornings are usually clear). There's a small grassy area perfect for sitting and taking it all in.

Leonard's Bakery — Malasadas
Food

A Honolulu institution since 1952. Leonard's makes the definitive Hawaiian malasada — a Portuguese-influenced sugar-dusted fried dough ball, filled or plain. The Pono (plain, hot from the fryer) is perfect, but the filled versions with haupia (coconut cream) or custard are next level. Expect a line. It's always worth it.

Order Get a half-dozen plain malasadas plus one haupia-filled and one custard-filled. They're best within 20 minutes of purchase. If you want to try the malasada truck, it roams Waikiki some mornings.

Waiola Shave Ice
Food

Hawaii's shave ice is not a snowcone — the ice is shaved to a cloud-like fine texture that soaks up the syrup differently. Waiola on Moiliili Street is a local favorite (and Oba­ma's reported stop of choice). The ice cream and azuki bean base underneath elevate it from a snack to a religious experience.

Order Get the large with an ice cream base and ask for azuki beans at the bottom. For flavors: li hing mui (salty-sour plum), passion fruit, and coconut are classics. The condensed milk drizzle on top is non-negotiable.
