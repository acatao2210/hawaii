Iolani Palace
Culture

The only official royal palace in the United States. Built in 1882 by King Kalakaua, it was home to Hawaii's last two monarchs before the illegal overthrow of the Hawaiian Kingdom in 1893. The self-guided audio tour takes you through the throne room, the King and Queen's chambers, and explains the heartbreaking story of Queen Lili'uokalani, who was held under house arrest here.

Book ahead Buy tickets online in advance at iolanipalace.org — they often sell out, especially on weekdays. Arrive early. Wear shoe covers are required inside (provided). Cash-only parking nearby.

Kualoa Ranch — ATV Tour
Must-do Hiking

One of the most cinematic landscapes on Oahu — you'll recognize it from Jurassic Park, Lost, and dozens of films. The ATV tour takes you deep into the Ko'olau mountains through jungle valleys and up to sweeping views of Kaneohe Bay. It's a 4,000-acre working cattle ranch that's been in the same Hawaiian family for over 150 years.

Book in advance Kualoa books up fast — reserve your ATV tour slot at kualoa.com at least 2–3 weeks ahead. The 'Best of Kualoa' combo tour is worth it. Wear clothes you don't mind getting dirty.

Sunset Beach + Waimea Bay
Beach

The North Shore in May is calm and beautiful — the massive winter swells are gone, leaving crystal-clear water perfect for swimming. Sunset Beach is famous for its winter surf competitions but in late spring it's a paradise for swimming and watching the sun dip below the horizon. Waimea Bay nearby has a jumping rock if you're feeling brave.

Timing Sunset in Honolulu in late May is around 7:15–7:30 PM. Get to Sunset Beach by 6 PM to find a good spot on the sand.

Giovanni's Shrimp Truck
Food

The most famous food truck on Oahu. Giovanni's has been parked in Kahuku on the North Shore since 1993, and the white truck covered in signatures is a Oahu institution. Get the garlic shrimp — a dozen prawns drowning in butter and roasted garlic, served over rice with lemon. It's messy, salty, and absolutely perfect.

The lineup There are several shrimp trucks in Kahuku — Giovanni's is the original with the signature graffiti-covered truck. There's often a line but it moves fast. Bring cash.
