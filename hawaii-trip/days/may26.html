Sea turtles in a hidden black-rock lagoon. The world's best coffee, grown on a volcano.

Check Out of Hilo + Drive to Kona
Culture

The drive from Hilo to Kona is one of the best road trips in Hawaii. You can take the southern route (Hwy 11 through Volcano and South Kona) — longer but dramatic, passing through Ka'u desert and remote coastline. Or the faster northern route (Hwy 19 via Waimea) through ranch country and lush upcountry. The southern route is recommended for scenery.

Route Saddle Road (Hwy 200) cuts straight across the island between the two volcanoes — dramatic high-altitude scenery and worth doing at least one way. It's no longer restricted for rental cars.

Kiholo Bay — Sea Turtle Lagoon
Beach Must-do

A hidden gem that requires a 2-mile round-trip walk along a lava coast to reach a pristine black-rock lagoon teeming with Hawaiian green sea turtles. You can snorkel here — the water is exceptionally clear and calm. Sea turtles are almost guaranteed. Go in the morning (before 2 PM) when the water is calmer.

Access Park at the small pullout on Queen Ka'ahumanu Hwy near mile marker 82. The trail heads west toward the ocean. Wear shoes — the lava is rough. No facilities. Bring water, snorkel gear, and lunch.

Kona Coffee Farm Tour
Culture Food

Kona coffee is among the rarest and most prized in the world — grown at elevation on the slopes of Hualalai volcano in rich volcanic soil and perfect cloud cover. Several farms offer free tours including Greenwell Farms (one of the oldest), Mountain Thunder, or Koa Coffee. You'll see the coffee cherry, learn the processing, and taste the difference between farm-direct and the blends in stores.

Buy direct Buy your coffee directly from the farm — prices are similar to stores but you get 100% Kona (many 'Kona blends' in stores are only 10% Kona). Greenwell Farms on Middle Keahole Rd is free and excellent.

Dinner in Kailua-Kona
Food Nightlife

Settle into your Kona accommodation and explore Alii Drive — the main strip along the waterfront in Kailua-Kona town. Huggo's on the Rocks is perfect for sunset drinks right on the ocean. For dinner, Jackie Rey's Ohana Grill does outstanding local fish (try the macadamia nut-crusted mahi mahi). Lemongrass Restaurant is excellent for Thai-influenced Pacific Rim cuisine.

Accommodation Stay in Kailua-Kona town for these two nights — you want to be close to the harbor for snorkeling access and Alii Drive. King Kamehameha's Kona Beach Hotel is right on the water. Airbnb options are plentiful in the surrounding area.
